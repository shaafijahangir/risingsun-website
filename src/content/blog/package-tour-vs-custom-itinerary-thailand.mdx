Package tours exist to solve a real problem: too many decisions, not enough confidence, and limited time to research a place you have never been.

Custom itineraries exist for a different reason: because the compromises built into a package deal — the group schedule, the pre-selected restaurants, the hotel that works for everyone and is therefore exactly right for no one — are not always worth what you pay to avoid them.

Both options have their place. Here is an honest comparison of what you get with each, and when each makes sense.

## What a Package Tour Gives You

A Thailand package from a major tour operator — think Kuoni, Exodus, Intrepid, or a flight+hotel bundle through a booking platform — typically includes:

- Return flights (or a flight option)
- Hotel accommodation at pre-contracted properties
- Airport transfers
- Some combination of guided tours, day trips, or excursions
- A customer service contact in case of problems

The pricing looks straightforward. It is not, because the package is assembled around what the operator can contract at volume, not around what is best for you specifically. The hotel was chosen because the operator has a commercial relationship with it. The restaurants on guided dinners were chosen the same way. The tour guide is whoever is available, not whoever is most knowledgeable about the specific places you are visiting.

None of this makes a package tour bad. It makes it a standardised product.

## What a Custom Itinerary Gives You

A custom Thailand itinerary built around your specific interests means:

- Hotels chosen because they are the right hotel for your dates, your budget, and your location priorities — not because someone has a commission arrangement
- A schedule that reflects your pace — whether that means two weeks of serious temples and street food or one beach day followed by two nothing days and a cooking class
- Private transport where it makes sense and public transport where it does not
- Restaurants recommended because they are excellent for the price, not because they pay for referrals
- A contact who has been to every place on your itinerary and can tell you what to actually do when you are there

The tradeoff is that it requires a conversation. You have to articulate what you want, which requires knowing enough to want something specific. This is where having an experienced local contact — rather than an algorithm — makes the difference.

## The Honest Price Comparison

Let me use a specific example: a 10-day Bangkok + Chiang Mai trip for two people in April.

**Package tour (major UK operator, per couple):**
- Flights: included (economy, indirect, two stops)
- Hotel: 4-star, central Bangkok + centrally-located Chiang Mai property
- Transfers: included
- 2 guided day tours: included
- Total listed price: £3,800–4,500 per couple

**Custom itinerary (same trip, same quality level):**
- Flights: booked separately, similar class, direct via Bangkok Air or Thai Airways: £900–1,100 per person = £1,800–2,200 per couple
- Hotels: comparable 4-star properties, booked directly or via reputable channels at similar or better rates: £80–120/night × 9 nights = £720–1,080
- Private airport transfers: £40–60 each way
- 2 private day tours (driver + guide, just the two of you): £90–140 per tour
- Total: £2,700–3,600 per couple

The custom version is typically £400–900 cheaper per couple for the same quality level. The gap widens as trip duration increases, because package overhead costs are distributed across fewer days for a 7-day trip than a 14-day one.

Where packages can win: last-minute bookings (operators sometimes discount heavily to fill unsold seats), complex multi-destination trips where the logistics overhead of booking everything independently is significant, and genuinely budget-focused trips where a package operator's volume contracts produce lower hotel pricing than independent booking.

## What Packages Cannot Do Well

**They cannot customise the pace.** A package tour schedule is a schedule. If you want to spend three extra days in Chiang Mai because it turned out to be extraordinary, you cannot. If the group tours move at a pace that does not suit you, you move at the group's pace.

**They cannot account for what you specifically value.** A food-obsessed traveller and a temple-obsessed traveller and a spa-and-pool traveller all get the same programme. One of them is perfectly served. The other two are not.

**They cannot access non-obvious options.** The restaurants, guesthouses, day trips, and local experiences that make Thailand extraordinary for someone who knows it well are not in any package brochure. They exist in the knowledge of someone who has been here thousands of times.

**They cannot flex around medical requirements.** For anyone combining a holiday with a medical appointment, consultation, or procedure in Bangkok, no package tour operator handles this. The logistics of coordinating hospital appointments, recovery time, and holiday activities require a different kind of contact.

## When a Package Makes Sense

**Your first trip to a complex destination** where the logistics of independent travel feel overwhelming. A package removes the decision paralysis.

**A trip where your primary goal is the destination, not the experience of travelling.** If you want to see the Grand Palace and eat Pad Thai and come home having done Thailand, a package delivers this reliably.

**Group travel** where herding ten people through independent logistics is not how you want to spend your holiday.

## When Custom Makes Sense

**Any trip where you know what you want** and a package's constraints would force compromises you do not want to make.

**Medical tourism.** No exceptions. A package operator cannot coordinate your hospital appointments, recovery stay, and leisure time around a procedure. This requires someone in Bangkok who understands both sides.

**Repeat visitors** who have done the package version and want something more.

**Anyone whose holiday preferences are specific** — a particular type of food, a particular kind of accommodation, a particular pace.
