For the right patient with the right condition, Bangkok's cardiac facilities are world-class. For some conditions, they are better than what patients can access at home: faster, better equipped, and staffed by surgeons with higher procedure volumes than many Western cardiac centres.

Here is what I tell people who ask.

## The Two Hospitals for Cardiac Cases

**Bumrungrad International Hospital: Heart Centre**

Bumrungrad's Heart Centre handles the full spectrum of cardiac cases: CABG, valve repair and replacement, structural heart disease, cardiac catheterisation, electrophysiology, and adult congenital conditions. The team includes cardiologists and surgeons trained at major institutions in the UK, US, and Germany.

Procedure volume matters in cardiac surgery. Bumrungrad's numbers are comparable to leading Western cardiac centres. The cardiac ICU nurse-to-patient ratio matches UK and US standards.

For most international patients with cardiac conditions, Bumrungrad is the first choice.

**Samitivej Sukhumvit: Heart and Vascular Institute**

Samitivej has particular strength in interventional cardiology: angioplasty, stenting, TAVI, and electrophysiology. For catheter-based interventions rather than open surgery, Samitivej is an excellent option and often faster to schedule for non-complex cases.

## Common Cardiac Procedures and Costs

**Coronary artery bypass (CABG):** South Asian patients have a significantly elevated genetic predisposition to coronary artery disease. CABG at Bumrungrad costs approximately $12,000–20,000 depending on complexity. The same procedure runs $80,000–150,000 in the US.

**Valve repair and replacement:** Mitral valve repair and aortic valve replacement are high-volume procedures at both hospitals. The right approach depends on a conversation with your specific surgeon.

**TAVI:** Transcatheter aortic valve implantation is available at both hospitals. Cost is approximately $18,000–28,000.

**Cardiac catheterisation and angiography:** Typically $1,500–3,500. A common diagnostic entry point before any intervention.

**Electrophysiology:** Atrial fibrillation ablation, pacemaker implantation, and ICD placement are handled by dedicated EP teams at both hospitals.

## Your Cardiologist at Home

Some home-country cardiologists will be supportive. They know Bumrungrad and Samitivej, they understand the cost reality, and they will help coordinate records and follow-up. Others will be cautious.

My recommendation: tell your cardiologist what you are considering. Ask them to review the Bangkok surgeon's credentials. Offer to arrange a direct consultation between your home cardiologist and the Bangkok team. Bangkok's hospitals run these international specialist consultations regularly for complex cases.

If your cardiologist reviews the credentials and still objects, that objection deserves serious weight. If they object without reviewing, it deserves less.

## Family During Cardiac Surgery

A CABG takes 3–6 hours. The wait is real.

Bumrungrad's cardiac surgery wing has a dedicated family waiting area. A coordinator updates family at intervals during the procedure. The surgeon speaks directly with family immediately after.

For family members who have flown to Bangkok, accommodation within five minutes of Bumrungrad is available at every price point along Sukhumvit Soi 1–11. I book this as part of every cardiac coordination.

## Recovery Timelines

- **CABG:** 5–7 days inpatient, then 2–4 weeks of recovery accommodation before flying. Most patients are cleared to fly at 4–6 weeks post-surgery.
- **Valve replacement:** Similar inpatient timeline. Mechanical valve patients need anticoagulation monitoring. Brief your home cardiologist before you travel.
- **Angioplasty or stenting:** Often same-day or one-night stay. Flying cleared within 1–2 weeks.
- **AF ablation:** 1–2 nights inpatient. Flying cleared within 1–2 weeks.

Discharge documentation from Bumrungrad's Heart Centre includes a full cardiac summary, medication list, and follow-up schedule formatted for your home cardiologist.
