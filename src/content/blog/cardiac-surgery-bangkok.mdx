Heart surgery is not a decision you make lightly.

Neither is choosing where to have it. The stakes are absolute. The margin for error is zero. And when a patient comes to me with a cardiac diagnosis and asks whether Bangkok is the right choice, I give them the same answer I give every client: the honest one, not the sales one.

For the right patient, with the right condition, Bangkok's cardiac facilities are genuinely world-class. For some conditions, they are better than what the patient can access at home — faster, better equipped, and staffed by surgeons with higher procedure volumes than many Western cardiac centres.

Here is what I tell people who ask.

## The Two Hospitals for Cardiac Cases

For international patients with serious cardiac conditions, the choice in Bangkok narrows to two institutions.

**Bumrungrad International Hospital — Heart Centre**

Bumrungrad's Heart Centre is one of Asia's highest-volume cardiac facilities. The centre handles the full spectrum of cardiac cases: coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG), valve repair and replacement, structural heart disease, cardiac catheterisation, electrophysiology, and congenital heart conditions in adults.

The Heart Centre is staffed by cardiologists and cardiac surgeons trained at major institutions in the UK, US, and Germany. Several have international fellowships and ongoing research affiliations. The centre performs thousands of cardiac procedures annually — procedure volume matters in cardiac surgery, and Bumrungrad's numbers are comparable to major Western cardiac centres.

The ICU standard is notable. Bumrungrad's cardiac ICU is equipped with current-generation monitoring and support technology, and the nurse-to-patient ratio in cardiac ICU is comparable to leading UK and US cardiac centres.

For most international patients with cardiac conditions, Bumrungrad is the first choice.

**Samitivej Sukhumvit — Heart & Vascular Institute**

Samitivej's Heart & Vascular Institute handles a significant cardiac volume, with particular strength in interventional cardiology — procedures such as angioplasty, stenting, TAVI (transcatheter aortic valve implantation), and electrophysiology. For patients who need catheter-based interventions rather than open surgery, Samitivej is an excellent option and is often slightly faster to schedule than Bumrungrad for non-complex interventional cases.

## Common Cardiac Conditions Treated by International Patients

**Coronary artery disease (CAD) and bypass surgery:** South Asian patients, particularly those from Bangladesh, Pakistan, and India, have a significantly elevated genetic predisposition to coronary artery disease. CABG at Bumrungrad is a frequently performed procedure for international patients. Costs are approximately $12,000–20,000 depending on the number of bypasses and complexity — versus $80,000–150,000 for the equivalent procedure in the US.

**Valve repair and replacement:** Mitral valve repair and aortic valve replacement are high-volume procedures at both hospitals. The choice between repair and replacement, and between tissue and mechanical valves, involves a conversation with your specific surgeon — not a general guide.

**TAVI:** Transcatheter aortic valve implantation — the catheter-based alternative to open valve replacement for high-risk surgical patients — is available at both hospitals. Cost is approximately $18,000–28,000.

**Cardiac catheterisation and angiography:** A common diagnostic procedure, also the entry point for interventional treatment. Typically $1,500–3,500 at Bangkok's international hospitals.

**Arrhythmia and electrophysiology:** Atrial fibrillation ablation, pacemaker implantation, and ICD placement are handled by dedicated electrophysiology teams at both hospitals.

## What Your Cardiologist at Home Will Think

This is the question that comes up in almost every cardiac consultation I have.

The honest answer is: it varies, and it depends on your cardiologist. Some will be supportive — they know the international reputation of Bumrungrad and Samitivej, they understand the cost reality for their patients, and they will help you coordinate records and follow-up care. Others will be cautious, either because they are unfamiliar with the specific hospitals or because they have professional reasons to prefer you stay local.

What I recommend to every cardiac patient: tell your cardiologist at home what you are considering and why. Ask them to review the credentials of the specific surgeon you would be seeing in Bangkok. Give them the hospital's international patient department contact and offer to arrange a direct consultation between your home cardiologist and the Bangkok team.

Bangkok's hospitals are used to this. International specialist consultations between the Bangkok team and home country cardiologists happen regularly, particularly for complex cases where ongoing monitoring is needed after the patient returns home.

If your cardiologist reviews the credentials and still objects, that objection deserves serious consideration. If they object without reviewing, that objection is less informative.

## The Family and Waiting During Cardiac Surgery

Cardiac procedures are long. A CABG takes 3–6 hours in theatre; the wait for family members is real.

Bumrungrad has a dedicated family waiting area for cardiac surgery. The patient coordinator keeps family updated at intervals during the procedure. The surgeon speaks directly with family immediately post-operatively.

For family members who have flown to Bangkok: accommodation close to Bumrungrad (within 5 minutes by car) is available at various price points. The area around Sukhumvit Soi 1–11 has options from budget guesthouses to serviced apartments. I book accommodation for the accompanying family members as part of every cardiac coordination.

## Recovery After Cardiac Surgery

Cardiac recovery timelines:
- **Coronary artery bypass:** Typically 5–7 days inpatient, followed by 2–4 weeks of recovery accommodation in Bangkok before flying home. Most patients are cleared to fly at 4–6 weeks post-surgery.
- **Valve replacement:** Similar inpatient timeline. Mechanical valve patients require anticoagulation monitoring — ensure your home cardiologist is briefed and ready to continue this.
- **Catheter-based procedures (angioplasty, stenting):** Often same-day or one-night stays. Flying typically cleared within 1–2 weeks.
- **Ablation (AF treatment):** 1–2 nights inpatient. Flying typically cleared within 1–2 weeks.

The discharge documentation from Bumrungrad's Heart Centre includes a full cardiac summary, medication list, and recommended follow-up schedule — formatted to international standards and ready to hand to your home cardiologist.
