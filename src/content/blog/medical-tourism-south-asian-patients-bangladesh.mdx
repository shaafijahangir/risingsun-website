Tens of thousands of patients from Bangladesh, India, and Pakistan travel to Bangkok for medical care every year. Bumrungrad International Hospital alone serves patients from over 190 countries, and South Asia consistently represents one of its largest patient groups.

This is not a new phenomenon. Bangkok has been a destination for South Asian medical tourists since the early 1990s, and the infrastructure built around serving this community — Bengali-speaking patient coordinators, Halal food options in hospital dining, specific cultural competence around South Asian health concerns — reflects three decades of experience.

Here is what the experience looks like for South Asian patients specifically, and why the numbers keep growing.

## Why Bangkok, Not Singapore or India

The question South Asian medical tourists most often ask is why Bangkok over the alternatives.

**Bangkok vs. Singapore:** Singapore has excellent hospitals — comparable quality to Bangkok's top tier. The difference is cost. A procedure at Bumrungrad costs 40–60% less than the equivalent at Mount Elizabeth or Gleneagles in Singapore. For a major surgery, this difference is tens of thousands of dollars.

**Bangkok vs. Apollo or Fortis in India:** India's private hospital sector has improved significantly and prices are competitive. Bangkok's advantages over India are: shorter wait times, consistently higher English proficiency at all levels of care, a more internationally diverse medical staff (many trained in the UK and US), and — for Bangladeshi and Pakistani patients specifically — easier visa access than many Indian hospital cities.

**Bangkok vs. home country:** For Bangladeshi patients in particular, this is the most relevant comparison. The gap in quality between Bangladesh's best private hospitals and Bangkok's JCI-accredited hospitals remains significant for complex procedures — particularly cardiac surgery, oncology, joint replacement, and fertility treatments. The cost differential, once flights and accommodation are included, is smaller than most people assume.

## The Language Situation for Bengali and Urdu Speakers

Bumrungrad Hospital has Bengali-speaking patient coordinators available during business hours. This is not a recent development — the hospital has served Bangladeshi patients since the early years of its international expansion and has built specific language infrastructure around this.

At Samitivej and BangPakok 9, English is the operating language of international patient services. Most Bangladeshi and Pakistani patients who have spent time in an English-medium education system manage this comfortably. Where language support is needed beyond English, translation services are available — though they require advance arrangement.

As a coordinator who has worked with Bangladeshi and South Asian patients for over two decades, I handle the communication logistics directly. The language barrier, in my experience, has rarely been the obstacle that patients fear it will be.

## Health Conditions That Are Particularly Relevant

South Asian patients, particularly from Bangladesh, India, and Pakistan, have elevated prevalence of several conditions that bring people to Bangkok:

**Cardiac conditions:** South Asians have a significantly higher genetic predisposition to coronary artery disease than other ethnic groups. Bypass surgery, valve repair, and cardiac catheterisation are among the most common procedures performed on South Asian patients at Bumrungrad's Heart Centre. The centre has decades of experience with this patient group.

**Type 2 diabetes and its complications:** Diabetic nephropathy, diabetic foot surgery, and eye complications related to diabetes are common referral reasons. Bangkok hospitals have endocrinology and metabolic disease centres that understand the South Asian diabetic profile.

**Orthopaedic surgery:** Joint replacement demand among older South Asian patients is high. BangPakok 9 in particular handles a significant volume of knee and hip replacements for South Asian patients.

**Oncology:** For cancer diagnoses requiring specialist treatment — prostate, breast, gastrointestinal cancers — Bumrungrad's cancer centre provides second opinions and treatment for South Asian patients who have received diagnoses at home.

**Fertility treatment:** IVF success rates at Bangkok fertility centres are among the highest in Asia. The relative affordability compared to UK or Australian fertility clinics ($4,500–6,000 per cycle versus £5,000–8,000+) makes Bangkok an important option for South Asian diaspora patients in the UK, Australia, and Canada.

## The Practical Details for Bangladeshi Patients

**Visa:** Thai tourist visas are available to Bangladeshi passport holders, either on arrival (30 days, at the airport) or in advance at the Thai Embassy in Dhaka (60 days). For medical purposes, a medical visa can be requested — I assist with this documentation for all clients.

**Flights:** Biman Bangladesh Airlines and Thai Airways operate direct flights between Dhaka and Bangkok. Return fares range from $200–450 depending on season and booking window. The flight is approximately 2 hours 40 minutes.

**Halal food:** Bumrungrad and Samitivej both offer Halal meal options for inpatients as standard — no special arrangement required. This is a consideration the hospitals have managed for decades.

**Prayer facilities:** Bumrungrad has a dedicated prayer room and understands Muslim prayer schedules. This is not an afterthought — it reflects the composition of their international patient base.

**Accompanying family:** South Asian patients typically travel with a family member. Thai hospitals are set up for this. Private rooms at all three recommended hospitals accommodate one accompanying person. For longer stays, serviced apartments in the Sukhumvit area (walking distance from Bumrungrad and Samitivej) can accommodate families of two to four comfortably.

## The Bangladesh-Bangkok Route for Medical Trips

The most efficient approach for a Bangladeshi patient travelling to Bangkok for planned medical care:

1. **Initial consultation via telemedicine.** Bumrungrad and Samitivej both offer international teleconsultation. Send your medical records, imaging, and history in advance. The consultant reviews and advises. This establishes whether Bangkok is the right option before any flights are booked.

2. **Fly in 1–2 days before the main appointment.** Jet lag is minimal on a 2h40m flight. A day to settle, confirm accommodation, and recover from travel is sufficient.

3. **Procedure and hospital stay.** Duration varies by procedure. Most orthopaedic cases are 5–7 days hospital stay. Cardiac surgery is longer.

4. **Recovery stay in Bangkok.** Depending on the procedure, 1–3 weeks of post-hospital recovery in nearby accommodation before flying home.

5. **Return to Dhaka.** Carry full discharge documentation. Arrange a follow-up with a doctor in Dhaka who can continue monitoring using the Bangkok hospital's notes.
