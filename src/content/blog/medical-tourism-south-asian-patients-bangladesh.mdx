Tens of thousands of patients from Bangladesh, India, and Pakistan travel to Bangkok for medical care every year. Bumrungrad International Hospital alone serves patients from over 190 countries, and South Asia is consistently one of its largest patient groups.

Bangkok has been a destination for South Asian medical tourists since the early 1990s. Bengali-speaking coordinators, Halal food in hospital dining, and cultural competence around South Asian health concerns reflect three decades of built infrastructure.

## Why Bangkok, Not Singapore or India

**Bangkok vs. Singapore:** Singapore has comparable clinical quality. The difference is cost. A procedure at Bumrungrad costs 40–60% less than at Mount Elizabeth or Gleneagles. On a major surgery, that gap is tens of thousands of dollars.

**Bangkok vs. Apollo or Fortis in India:** India's private hospitals have improved and prices are competitive. Bangkok's advantages are shorter wait times, consistently high English proficiency at all care levels, internationally trained staff, and easier visa access for Bangladeshi and Pakistani patients than many Indian hospital cities.

**Bangkok vs. home country:** For Bangladeshi patients, this is the most relevant comparison. The quality gap between Bangladesh's best private hospitals and Bangkok's JCI-accredited hospitals remains significant for cardiac surgery, oncology, joint replacement, and fertility treatment. The cost differential, once flights and accommodation are included, is smaller than most people assume.

## Language

Bumrungrad has Bengali-speaking patient coordinators available during business hours. This is not new. The hospital has served Bangladeshi patients since its early international expansion and has built specific language infrastructure around it.

At Samitivej and BangPakok 9, the operating language for international patients is English. Most Bangladeshi and Pakistani patients from English-medium education backgrounds manage this comfortably. Translation services are available with advance arrangement.

As a coordinator who has worked with South Asian patients for over two decades, I handle communication logistics directly. Language has rarely been the obstacle patients fear it will be.

## Health Conditions Most Relevant to South Asian Patients

**Cardiac conditions:** South Asians have a significantly higher genetic predisposition to coronary artery disease. Bypass surgery, valve repair, and cardiac catheterisation are among the most common procedures at Bumrungrad's Heart Centre for this patient group.

**Type 2 diabetes and complications:** Diabetic nephropathy, diabetic foot surgery, and eye complications are common referral reasons. Bangkok's endocrinology centres understand the South Asian diabetic profile.

**Orthopaedic surgery:** Knee and hip replacement demand among older South Asian patients is high. BangPakok 9 handles significant volume for this group.

**Oncology:** For prostate, breast, and gastrointestinal cancers, Bumrungrad's cancer centre provides second opinions and treatment for patients who have received diagnoses at home.

**Fertility treatment:** IVF success rates at Bangkok fertility centres are among the highest in Asia. At $4,500–6,000 per cycle versus £5,000–8,000+ in the UK, Bangkok is an important option for South Asian diaspora patients.

## Practical Details for Bangladeshi Patients

**Visa:** Thai tourist visas are available on arrival (30 days) or in advance from the Thai Embassy in Dhaka (60 days). For medical purposes, a medical visa can be requested. I assist with this documentation for all clients.

**Flights:** Biman Bangladesh Airlines and Thai Airways operate direct flights between Dhaka and Bangkok. Return fares run $200–450 depending on season. The flight is 2 hours 40 minutes.

**Halal food:** Bumrungrad and Samitivej both offer Halal inpatient meal options as standard. No special arrangement is required.

**Prayer facilities:** Bumrungrad has a dedicated prayer room and understands Muslim prayer schedules. This reflects the composition of their patient base, not an afterthought.

**Accompanying family:** South Asian patients typically travel with a family member. All three recommended hospitals accommodate one companion in private rooms. For longer stays, serviced apartments in Sukhumvit comfortably accommodate families of two to four.

## The Bangladesh-Bangkok Route

The most efficient approach for a Bangladeshi patient:

1. **Telemedicine first.** Bumrungrad and Samitivej both offer international teleconsultation. Send your records, imaging, and history in advance. Confirm Bangkok is the right option before booking any flights.
2. **Arrive 1–2 days before your appointment.** Jet lag is minimal on a short flight. One day to settle is enough.
3. **Procedure and hospital stay.** Most orthopaedic cases are 5–7 days. Cardiac surgery is longer.
4. **Recovery stay in Bangkok.** Depending on the procedure, 1–3 weeks post-hospital before flying home.
5. **Return to Dhaka with full documentation.** Arrange follow-up with a doctor in Dhaka who can continue monitoring using the Bangkok hospital's notes.
