For cardiac surgery, major orthopaedic work, and complex oncology treatment, having a family member present is clinically beneficial. It reduces patient anxiety, improves medication adherence, and means post-operative instructions are absorbed by two people rather than one.

Practically: when you are discharged and cannot manage your own luggage, someone manages it. When you need food but cannot easily get it, someone gets it. When you want to say you are scared without saying it to a coordinator, someone understands.

Here is how to plan a two-person medical trip well.

## Accommodation

For a solo patient, you optimise for medical proximity, elevator access, and room service. For two people, you also optimise for the companion's livability during the 5-7 days you are in hospital.

**Private hospital room:** All three major international hospitals (Bumrungrad, Samitivej, BangPakok 9) offer private rooms with a fold-out or rollaway bed for one companion. The companion has bathroom access and can eat from the hospital restaurant. For the initial hospital stay, this is the right setup. The companion is on-site, which matters most in the first 24-48 hours after surgery.

**Serviced apartment for recovery:** Once you are discharged, a hotel room adequate for one person becomes cramped for two. A one-bedroom serviced apartment in Sukhumvit, around Soi 1-11 for Bumrungrad patients, provides a proper bed for the companion, a separate space for rest, a small kitchen, and a living area. Suitable apartments typically cost $80-150 per night and are significantly more comfortable than hotel rooms at the same price.

## What the Companion Actually Does

**During the hospital stay:** The companion's primary role is presence. Being there when you wake from surgery. Being there the first evening when the anaesthesia has worn off. Being there to receive post-operative instructions when you are not in a state to absorb them.

Beyond presence: communicating preferences to nursing staff, keeping family at home updated, managing personal belongings during the transition to recovery accommodation, and handling paperwork. The international patient coordinators manage most practical logistics. The companion does not need to be the liaison with the hospital system.

**During free time in Bangkok:** The companion will have significant free time while you are in hospital and resting. Within 20 minutes of Bumrungrad and Samitivej:

- Chatuchak Weekend Market (Saturday and Sunday), one of Asia's largest markets
- The Chao Phraya river: river express boat, Wat Pho, the Grand Palace area
- Ari neighbourhood, five minutes north by BTS, with good coffee shops and local restaurants
- Or Tor Kor Market, the city's best fresh produce market, adjacent to Chatuchak
- Traditional Thai massage at the right places, an hour in the afternoon

Most companions come home from a medical trip having had a better time than expected.

## The Combined Budget for Two People

A realistic two-person budget for a three-week medical trip (procedure plus recovery):

| Item | Estimated cost (USD) |
|------|----------------------|
| Return flights, 2 people, economy, UK example | $1,400-2,200 |
| Hospital private room, 7 nights, companion bed included | Included in hospital costs |
| Serviced apartment, 14 nights, 1-bedroom | $1,100-2,100 |
| Food for two, mix of restaurant and room service | $600-900 |
| Companion's Bangkok activities and transport | $300-500 |
| Shared transport, airport and follow-up appointments | $150-250 |
| **Total additional cost of bringing a companion** | **$2,500-4,000** |

The companion adds cost, but not as much as most people assume. Flights are the largest variable. If the companion uses points, works remotely, or was planning an Asia trip anyway, the net cost reduces significantly.

## Planning the Companion's Experience

The best two-person medical trips are ones where the companion has a light itinerary built in advance. Not because the trip is about them. Because companions who have nothing to do become anxious and restless, which is not good for either of you.
