After discharge, most patients are well enough to move around but not ready to fly. That window lasts one to two weeks. You are in one of the most interesting cities in the world. It is entirely possible to fill that time well.

Here is a realistic 10-day itinerary built around a knee replacement. Adjust the procedure and timeline for your situation. The structure applies broadly.

## Before the Procedure

Arrive 1–2 days early. You need to adjust to the timezone, eat a proper meal, confirm pre-operative instructions, and arrive at the hospital rested.

Use the arrival day to check in to your serviced apartment and confirm the hospital registration process. The area around Bumrungrad, Sukhumvit Soi 3–15, has excellent Bengali, Middle Eastern, and international restaurants within walking distance. This is your last meal before surgery. Make it count.

## Days 1–5: Hospital

Procedure and inpatient stay. This part is managed by the hospital.

Your companion, if you brought one, has Bangkok available. Chatuchak Weekend Market, the Chao Phraya river, the National Museum, and the restaurants of Ari are all within an hour. They should use this time.

## Days 6–8: Early Recovery

You are back in the serviced apartment. You are mobile with a crutch or frame. The acute phase is over, but rest is still essential.

What works: breakfast at an accessible nearby café, morning physiotherapy, afternoon rest, evenings with your leg elevated. One gentle outing is usually possible by day 7 or 8. A short taxi to a riverside restaurant or a slow walk through a nearby market. Not sightseeing. A beginning.

## Days 9–12: Active Recovery

Most knee replacement patients at this stage are walking 500m–1km without stopping. Pain is manageable. The shift from patient to traveller starts here.

**Wat Pho** is one of the most accessible major temples in Bangkok for limited mobility. Ground level, wide spaces, seating available. The reclining Buddha is extraordinary. A slow 90-minute visit followed by riverside lunch is a realistic day.

**Jim Thompson's House** is a quiet shaded garden estate in the Silom area, minimal walking required. An hour here plus lunch is comfortable.

**A Chao Phraya river boat trip**, public express or private longtail, is entirely manageable and shows you a different face of the city. Most boats have seating and the journey is smooth.

**Spa.** Post-operative therapeutic massage of unaffected areas, shoulders, neck, back, lower legs, is appropriate by week 2. Swedish-style or oil massage, not traditional Thai massage at this stage. Most good spas nearby offer both.

## Days 13–14: Pre-Departure

Final physiotherapy and a surgeon review. This is where you get your fitness-to-fly clearance, discharge documentation, and follow-up instructions.

The day before departure: pack, rest, eat well. The flight home after knee replacement is manageable but genuinely tiring. Treat the day before as preparation.

## What the Trip Adds Up To

Ten days in Bangkok. Five in hospital, five in a recovery that gradually opens into an actual experience of the city. You come home with your procedure done, recovery well-managed, and a relationship with Bangkok that is nothing like an average tourist visit.

The cost of the recovery portion, serviced apartment, physiotherapy, and outings, is typically less than equivalent private aftercare at home.
