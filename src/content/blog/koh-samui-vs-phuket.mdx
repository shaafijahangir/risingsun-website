Koh Samui or Phuket is the most common island question I get. The answer comes down to three things.

## The Three Questions

**1. Do you want a large island with full infrastructure, or a smaller one that feels contained?**

Phuket is about 543 square kilometres with a direct international airport, major resorts, shopping centres, and hospitals. The quiet beaches and the party beaches are miles apart and barely notice each other.

Koh Samui is about 228 square kilometres. It has its own airport, though most visitors connect through Bangkok. You can drive around it in an hour. The pace is slower, and the sense of arrival is stronger.

**2. Does beach quality matter more, or overall infrastructure?**

Phuket's best beaches, Kata Noi, Kamala, and Natai in the north, are among the best in Thailand. Patong on a busy weekend is chaotic and overcrowded. The variance is large.

Koh Samui's beaches are consistently good to excellent. Chaweng is lively and developed. Bophut and Mae Nam on the north coast are quieter and genuinely beautiful. Bang Kao in the south is largely undiscovered. The floor is higher.

**3. Do you want nightlife accessible, or out of the way?**

Patong in Phuket is one of Southeast Asia's most intense nightlife districts. It is also entirely avoidable. Beautiful resorts in Kamala or Rawai exist in a different world from Patong.

Koh Samui's nightlife centres on Chaweng. Livelier than many expect for a smaller island, but not Patong. Staying in Bophut makes Chaweng feel like a distant rumour.

## Who Phuket Is For

First-time visitors who want the full infrastructure package. Reliable restaurants at every price point, dive operations, day trips to Phi Phi and Phang Nga Bay, international hospitals if needed, and a direct flight home.

Groups with mixed interests. The size of Phuket means the beach contingent and the sightseers and the spa-and-quiet-dinner people can all have completely different days from the same resort.

Divers. The Similan Islands, one of the world's premier dive destinations, are accessible from Khao Lak, a 90-minute drive north of Phuket.

Anyone making a side trip to Phi Phi Islands. Phi Phi is a day trip from Phuket and much harder to reach from Koh Samui.

## Who Koh Samui Is For

Couples looking for a contained, romantic holiday. Koh Samui has an exceptional concentration of boutique resorts, particularly on the quieter north and south coasts, that offer a quality of experience Phuket matches only at its most expensive properties.

Families with younger children. Calmer sea conditions on the north coast during much of the year, smaller distances, and a generally less frenetic atmosphere make it easier with kids.

Repeat visitors who have done Phuket. Koh Samui rewards more exploration and knowledge of where the good things are.

Anyone adding Koh Phangan or Koh Tao. These smaller islands are a short ferry from Koh Samui and form a natural three-island itinerary.

## What Both Are Bad At

Neither island is Bangkok. For anyone coming to Thailand primarily for temples, markets, and street food culture, the islands are add-ons. A week in Bangkok plus four days on an island beats ten days on an island for most first-timers.

Neither island is cheap compared to mainland Thailand. Resort accommodation on both is international pricing. Street food remains excellent value, but the islands strain a tight budget more than Bangkok does.

## The Third Option

Krabi.

Krabi mainland (Ao Nang) and its surrounding islands, including Railay Beach, Koh Lanta, and Phi Phi from the Krabi side, offer dramatic limestone karst scenery with beach quality that matches either island. Less developed than Phuket's tourist areas, less remote than Koh Samui. Accessible by air from Bangkok in about 75 minutes. Spectacular.

If you have done both Phuket and Koh Samui, Krabi is what I recommend next.

## The Seasonal Factor

Thai island weather is seasonal and differs between the two coasts.

**Phuket and Krabi (Andaman coast):** Best November–April. Monsoon May–October with rain, rough seas, and some resort closures.

**Koh Samui and Koh Phangan (Gulf of Thailand coast):** Best February–September. Heavy rain comes October–January. A December trip to Koh Samui is genuinely risky from a weather standpoint.

If you are visiting in December, Phuket is the correct answer regardless of the Samui-versus-Phuket comparison.
