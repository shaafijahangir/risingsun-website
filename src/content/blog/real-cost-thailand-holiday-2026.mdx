Thailand remains one of the world's best-value travel destinations. It is also not as cheap as it was ten years ago. Some corners of it are priced for people who stopped checking the exchange rate.

The gap between what a careful traveller spends and what an incautious one spends is larger in Thailand than almost anywhere else. Here are actual figures for 2026.

All prices in USD. Exchange rate approximately 36 THB to 1 USD at time of writing.

## Budget Travel: $40–70 Per Person Per Day

**Accommodation:** $15–25/night. In Bangkok, this gets a clean air-conditioned room with private bathroom in Silom or Ari. On Koh Samui's quieter beaches, similar money gets a fan bungalow with shared facilities.

**Food:** $10–15/day. Pad Thai from a street stall is 60–80 baht (under $2.50). A full meal at a local restaurant is 100–150 baht. Breakfast and coffee comes to about $5/day.

**Transport:** $5–8/day. BTS and MRT in Bangkok. Songthaews on the islands at 30–50 baht. Grab for longer journeys.

**Activities:** $5–15/day. Temple entry is free or minimal. National parks cost $5–8. A half-day cooking class runs $25–35. A day trip to Ayutthaya from Bangkok, including transport, is $20–30.

**A 10-day budget trip:** $500–750 per person, excluding flights.

---

## Mid-Range Travel: $100–180 Per Person Per Day

**Accommodation:** $50–100/night. A three-star hotel in central Bangkok with pool access and a proper breakfast. On the islands, a boutique resort with a sea view and a reasonable beach.

**Food:** $30–50/day. Bangkok has exceptional value at this level. You can eat extremely well at $15–25 per meal including wine, at restaurants that would cost three times as much in London or Sydney.

**Transport:** $15–20/day. Private taxi for most Bangkok journeys, occasional BTS, private airport transfer.

**Activities:** $20–40/day. Guided half-day tours, day trips with a private driver.

**A 10-day mid-range trip:** $1,200–2,000 per person, excluding flights.

---

## Comfortable and Luxury Travel: $250–600+ Per Person Per Day

**Accommodation:** $150–400/night. Bangkok has properties including The Capella, Rosewood, and the Mandarin Oriental that rank among the finest hotels in the world, priced at 40–60% of what equivalent quality costs in London, Paris, or New York. On Koh Samui, a private pool villa at a Ritz-Carlton or Six Senses property runs $300–600/night.

**Food:** $80–150/day. A tasting menu at a top Bangkok restaurant costs $60–100 per person, a fraction of equivalent quality in Western capitals.

**Transport:** $30–60/day. Private car on call, occasional boat charter.

**Activities:** $50–150/day. Private tours, chartered day boats, cooking classes with top chefs, premium spa.

**A 10-day luxury trip:** $3,000–6,500 per person, excluding flights.

## What Most Travellers Get Wrong

**Hotel transport.** Metered taxis and Grab are cheaper than anything your hotel arranges, with one exception: airport arrivals. A private pickup is worth arranging. The metered taxi queue at Suvarnabhumi can run 30 minutes at peak hours.

**Hotel restaurants.** Hotel restaurants in Bangkok are consistently overpriced. Walk five minutes in any direction in Sukhumvit and the food improves while the price drops.

**Over-scheduling.** The days you remember in Thailand are not the ones with three activities booked. Budget empty time.

**The Songkran effect.** If you are visiting in April, check whether your dates overlap with Songkran (Thai New Year water festival, usually 13–15 April, with extended celebrations across the week). Accommodation prices double, streets in key areas are flooded with water, and transport is limited. Extraordinary if you want it, chaotic if you do not.
