The NHS wait list for knee replacement in England is currently 12–18 months for most patients. In the United States, the surgery costs $40,000–50,000 out of pocket if you are uninsured or facing a high deductible. In Australia, private hospital waits run 6–12 months even with insurance.

In Bangkok, at a JCI-accredited hospital with an internationally trained orthopaedic surgeon, the same procedure costs $10,000–16,000 all-in. Not the surgery alone — the total cost of the procedure, the hospital stay, and the post-operative physiotherapy.

This post gives you the real numbers: what the surgery costs at each of the three hospitals I recommend, what else you need to budget for, and what a realistic three-week trip to Bangkok actually costs in total.

## What Knee Replacement Costs at Each Hospital

These are 2026 price ranges based on standard total knee replacement (TKR). Bilateral (both knees) is roughly 1.6× the single-knee cost, not double — there is shared anaesthetic and theatre time.

**Bumrungrad International Hospital**
- Total knee replacement (one knee): $14,000–17,000
- This includes: surgeon's fee, anaesthesia, theatre time, implant (Zimmer Biomet or Stryker), 4–5 night hospital stay, post-op physiotherapy sessions during admission
- Implant choice affects cost — standard implant vs. a premium brand adds $1,500–2,500

**Samitivej Sukhumvit Hospital**
- Total knee replacement (one knee): $12,000–15,000
- Comparable implant quality to Bumrungrad; slightly lower overhead costs
- Strong orthopaedic department, shorter wait time for consultation appointments

**BangPakok 9 International Hospital**
- Total knee replacement (one knee): $9,000–12,000
- The best-value option. Same implant manufacturers, JCI accreditation, fixed-price packages published on request
- Particularly well-regarded for orthopaedic volume — they perform a high number of TKRs per year, which matters for surgical outcome consistency

All three prices include the implant and the hospital stay. None include the items below, which is where most people underestimate the full cost.

## What People Forget to Budget

**Accommodation near the hospital**
You will need to stay somewhere for 2–3 weeks after discharge. You cannot be far from a hospital in case of complications, and you cannot manage stairs for the first week. A good option is a serviced apartment or hotel within 10–15 minutes of your hospital.

Budget: $50–120 per night depending on comfort level. For a 16-night stay: $800–1,920.

**Post-discharge physiotherapy**
Your in-hospital physio gets you standing and walking. What happens in weeks 2–3 determines your long-term outcome. Daily physiotherapy sessions from a qualified therapist run $40–80 per session in Bangkok, far less than equivalent care at home.

Budget: $500–900 for 10–12 post-discharge sessions.

**Medication**
Blood thinners (DVT prevention), pain management, and anti-inflammatory medication for the first 2–3 weeks. Bangkok hospitals dispense this on discharge.

Budget: $150–250.

**Ground transport**
You will not be taking the BTS Skytrain with a freshly replaced knee. Private car transport to appointments and outings for three weeks.

Budget: $300–500.

**Flights**
From the UK, return flights to Bangkok range from £450 (budget carrier) to £900 (full service). You will want to book a direct flight home with aisle seating — budget airlines and middle seats are not advisable 2–3 weeks post-surgery.

From the US East Coast: $900–1,500 return. From Australia: AUD 900–1,600.

Note: some patients upgrade to business class for the flight home. With a swollen knee and limited mobility, this is a reasonable investment. Budget accordingly if it applies to you.

## What the Surgery Actually Involves

This is not a day procedure. Total knee replacement is major surgery under general or spinal anaesthesia, lasting 1.5–2 hours. You will be in hospital for 4–6 nights. The first 48 hours are the most uncomfortable — pain is managed with a combination of nerve blocks, epidural, and oral analgesics.

By day 3–4, most patients are standing with a frame and taking a few steps. By day 7, you are walking short distances with a crutch. By week 3, most patients with standard recovery can manage a long-haul flight home with appropriate preparation.

The recovery at 3 months, 6 months, and 1 year is determined more by post-operative physiotherapy than by the surgery itself. What Bangkok gives you is both the surgery at a fraction of the home price *and* affordable daily physio throughout recovery.

## The Implant Question

Some patients arrive having researched implant brands. The major manufacturers — Zimmer Biomet, Stryker, DePuy Synthes — are available at all three hospitals above. These are the same companies supplying NHS trusts and US surgical centres.

Cheaper implants from lesser-known manufacturers do exist in Thailand. They are used at lower-tier facilities. The hospitals I recommend do not use them. Ask specifically which implant brand will be used when you receive your quote.
