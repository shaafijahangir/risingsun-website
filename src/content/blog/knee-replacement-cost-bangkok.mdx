The NHS wait for knee replacement is 12–18 months. In the US, it costs $40,000–50,000 out of pocket. In Bangkok, at a JCI-accredited hospital with an internationally trained orthopaedic surgeon, the same procedure runs $10,000–16,000 all-in.

That figure includes the surgery, the hospital stay, and post-operative physiotherapy during admission. Here is the full breakdown.

## Surgery Costs by Hospital (2026)

These are standard total knee replacement (TKR) ranges. Bilateral (both knees) is roughly 1.6x the single-knee price, not double, because anaesthetic and theatre time are shared.

**Bumrungrad International Hospital**
- Single TKR: $14,000–17,000
- Includes: surgeon, anaesthesia, theatre, implant (Zimmer Biomet or Stryker), 4–5 night stay, in-hospital physio
- Premium implant adds $1,500–2,500

**Samitivej Sukhumvit Hospital**
- Single TKR: $12,000–15,000
- Comparable implant quality, slightly lower overheads, shorter consultation wait times

**BangPakok 9 International Hospital**
- Single TKR: $9,000–12,000
- Best value option. Same implant manufacturers. JCI-accredited. Fixed-price packages available.
- High TKR volume, which matters for surgical outcome consistency

All prices include implant and hospital stay. The items below are where most people underestimate the total.

## What People Forget to Budget

**Accommodation (16 nights post-discharge):** Serviced apartment or hotel within 15 minutes of the hospital. $50–120 per night. Budget $800–1,920.

**Post-discharge physiotherapy (10–12 sessions):** The in-hospital sessions get you standing. The sessions in weeks 2–3 determine your long-term outcome. $40–80 per session in Bangkok. Budget $500–900.

**Medication:** Blood thinners, pain management, anti-inflammatories for 2–3 weeks. Dispensed on discharge. Budget $150–250.

**Ground transport:** No BTS Skytrain with a fresh knee replacement. Private car for three weeks. Budget $300–500.

**Flights:** UK return: £450–900. US East Coast return: $900–1,500. Australia return: AUD 900–1,600. Book a direct flight home with an aisle seat. Consider business class for the journey home if mobility is significantly limited.

## What the Surgery Involves

Total knee replacement is major surgery. General or spinal anaesthesia, 1.5–2 hours in theatre, 4–6 nights in hospital. The first 48 hours are the most uncomfortable, managed with nerve blocks, epidural, and oral analgesics.

By day 3–4, most patients are standing with a frame. By day 7, walking short distances with a crutch. By week 3, most patients with standard recovery can manage a long-haul flight home with appropriate preparation.

Long-term outcome is determined more by post-operative physiotherapy than by the surgery itself. Bangkok gives you both the surgery at a fraction of the home price and affordable daily physio throughout recovery.

## The Implant Question

The major manufacturers: Zimmer Biomet, Stryker, DePuy Synthes. All available at the three hospitals above. These are the same companies supplying NHS trusts and US surgical centres. Cheaper implants from lesser-known manufacturers exist at lower-tier facilities. The hospitals I recommend do not use them. Ask specifically which brand will be used when you receive your quote.
