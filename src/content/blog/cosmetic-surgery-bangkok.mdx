Bangkok has a cosmetic surgery problem — not the quality, but the reputation.

A handful of cut-price clinics, operating out of shopfronts and tourist-facing facilities, have made headlines that the serious hospitals do not deserve. If your understanding of cosmetic surgery in Bangkok comes from tabloid coverage or horror stories shared on social media, it is almost certainly drawn from that category — not from the JCI-accredited hospitals where the majority of medical tourists are actually treated.

Here is the real picture: what legitimate cosmetic surgery in Bangkok looks like, what it costs, and what makes it worth considering.

## The Two Tiers of Cosmetic Surgery in Bangkok

This is the most important thing to understand before anything else.

**Tier 1: JCI-accredited hospitals with dedicated plastic surgery departments.** These include Bumrungrad, Samitivej, and Yanhee Hospital (which specialises in cosmetic and reconstructive surgery). Surgeons have verifiable credentials — Thai Board of Plastic Surgery certification at minimum, often plus fellowships in the UK, US, or Japan. The facilities, anaesthetic protocols, and aftercare are indistinguishable from a private hospital in London or Sydney.

**Tier 2: Standalone cosmetic clinics.** Some of these are perfectly competent. Many are not. They operate with lower overheads, less rigorous surgeon screening, and limited capacity to manage complications. They are cheaper for a reason.

Everything in this post refers to Tier 1.

## What Procedures Cost (2026 Figures)

**Rhinoplasty (nose reshaping)**
- UK/US price: £4,000–8,000 / $7,000–12,000
- Bangkok (accredited hospital): $2,500–4,500
- Recovery: 7–10 days of visible bruising/swelling, presentable in 2–3 weeks, final result at 12 months
- What to know: Thai surgeons have particular expertise in rhinoplasty for Asian facial structure — both for South East Asian patients and for Western patients seeking specific structural changes

**Blepharoplasty (eyelid surgery)**
- UK/US price: £2,500–5,000 / $4,000–7,000
- Bangkok (accredited hospital): $1,500–3,000
- Recovery: swelling and bruising for 10–14 days, final result in 4–6 weeks
- What to know: one of the most commonly performed procedures in Bangkok; surgeons here have high volume and strong outcomes data

**Breast augmentation**
- UK/US price: £5,000–8,000 / $7,000–12,000
- Bangkok (accredited hospital): $4,000–6,500
- Recovery: 5–7 days of restricted movement, return to light activity at 2 weeks, full recovery at 6–8 weeks
- Implants: Allergan and Mentor (the same brands used in the UK and US) are available. Confirm the implant brand in your pre-op consultation.

**Abdominoplasty (tummy tuck)**
- UK/US price: £6,000–10,000 / $9,000–15,000
- Bangkok (accredited hospital): $4,500–7,000
- Recovery: the most significant of the procedures listed. 2–3 weeks minimum before flying; limited mobility for the first week. Plan for a longer Bangkok stay.

**Liposuction (per area)**
- UK/US price: £3,000–5,000 / $4,500–7,500
- Bangkok (accredited hospital): $2,000–4,000
- Recovery: varies by area and volume. 1–2 weeks of compression garment wear. Most patients fly home at 10–14 days.

## What the Process Looks Like

The consultation is the first thing, and it matters. A legitimate Bangkok plastic surgeon does not rush the consultation. Expect 30–45 minutes covering: your medical history, what you want to achieve, what is and is not realistic, the specific technique they will use, and a full breakdown of costs.

If a consultation lasts ten minutes and ends with a price, that is a warning sign.

What happens next: pre-operative blood work and a clearance check from the anaesthesiologist (at accredited hospitals, this is standard — not optional). Surgery is scheduled 1–2 days later in most cases.

Post-operatively, you will have wound checks, compression garments for relevant procedures, and a discharge consultation with written aftercare instructions in English.

## The Recovery Stay Question

This is where most cosmetic surgery tourists underestimate the planning.

You cannot recover in a standard hotel. Stairs are difficult with a tummy tuck. You need somewhere with room service or easy food access, good blackout curtains, easy bathroom access, and ideally a pool for later-stage recovery. A serviced apartment or a hotel with good accessibility within 15 minutes of your hospital is the right solution.

For rhinoplasty or eyelid surgery, 10–14 days in Bangkok before flying home is appropriate. For abdominoplasty or breast augmentation, 2–3 weeks is safer. For liposuction, most patients fly at 10–12 days.

## What You Cannot Do Differently in Bangkok

Cosmetic surgery is elective. Complications — while uncommon at accredited hospitals — do happen, and they happen the same way they would at home: the most common issues are infection, asymmetry, and unsatisfactory results that need revision.

The difference in Bangkok is that you are not at home when it happens. This is manageable with the right preparation: travel insurance that covers cosmetic complications (not all policies do — ask specifically), a Bangkok surgeon who is available for follow-up consultations via telemedicine, and clear instructions on what your home GP should look for on return.

None of this is a reason not to go. It is a reason to plan carefully.

## What Makes Bangkok Worth It

The savings are real and significant — typically 50–60% lower than equivalent private care in the UK or Australia, and 60–70% lower than the US.

Beyond cost, Bangkok at the serious hospital level offers high surgical volume (Thai plastic surgeons often perform 5–10× more procedures per year than their Western counterparts, which matters for outcome quality), access to advanced techniques that some Western hospitals have slower adoption of, and a recovery environment where professional aftercare is affordable on a daily basis.

The experience of recovering in Bangkok — with good food, professional massage available, and a warm climate — is not the worst way to spend two weeks. Many patients combine the procedure with a gentle holiday in the weeks after discharge.
