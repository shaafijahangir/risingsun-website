Bangkok's cosmetic surgery reputation suffers from a small number of cut-price clinics that attract headlines the serious hospitals do not deserve. At JCI-accredited facilities, the standard of care is equivalent to private hospitals in London or Sydney. The price is 50–60% lower.

Here is what legitimate cosmetic surgery in Bangkok looks like.

## Two Tiers. Only One Worth Considering.

**JCI-accredited hospitals with dedicated plastic surgery departments.** Bumrungrad, Samitivej, and Yanhee Hospital (which specialises in cosmetic and reconstructive surgery). Surgeons hold Thai Board of Plastic Surgery certification at minimum, often with fellowships in the UK, US, or Japan.

**Standalone cosmetic clinics.** Some are competent. Many are not. Lower overheads, less rigorous surgeon screening, limited capacity to manage complications. Cheaper for a reason.

Everything below refers to accredited hospitals only.

## What Procedures Cost (2026)

**Rhinoplasty**
- UK/US: £4,000–8,000 / $7,000–12,000
- Bangkok (accredited): $2,500–4,500
- Recovery: 7–10 days of visible bruising, presentable at 2–3 weeks, final result at 12 months
- Thai surgeons have high volume and specific expertise in rhinoplasty for both Asian and Western facial structure

**Blepharoplasty (eyelid surgery)**
- UK/US: £2,500–5,000 / $4,000–7,000
- Bangkok (accredited): $1,500–3,000
- Recovery: bruising and swelling for 10–14 days, final result at 4–6 weeks
- One of the most commonly performed procedures in Bangkok; strong outcomes data

**Breast augmentation**
- UK/US: £5,000–8,000 / $7,000–12,000
- Bangkok (accredited): $4,000–6,500
- Recovery: restricted movement for 5–7 days, light activity at 2 weeks, full recovery at 6–8 weeks
- Allergan and Mentor implants available. Confirm the brand in your pre-op consultation.

**Abdominoplasty (tummy tuck)**
- UK/US: £6,000–10,000 / $9,000–15,000
- Bangkok (accredited): $4,500–7,000
- Recovery: 2–3 weeks minimum before flying. Plan for a longer Bangkok stay than other procedures.

**Liposuction (per area)**
- UK/US: £3,000–5,000 / $4,500–7,500
- Bangkok (accredited): $2,000–4,000
- Recovery: 1–2 weeks compression garment wear. Most patients fly home at 10–14 days.

## What the Process Looks Like

The consultation comes first. A legitimate Bangkok plastic surgeon takes 30–45 minutes covering your medical history, what you want to achieve, what is and is not realistic, the specific technique, and a full cost breakdown. A ten-minute consultation that ends with a price is a warning sign.

Pre-operative blood work and anaesthesiologist clearance are standard at accredited hospitals, not optional. Surgery is typically scheduled 1–2 days after consultation.

Post-operatively: wound checks, compression garments for relevant procedures, and written aftercare instructions in English at discharge.

## Plan Your Recovery Stay

Standard hotels do not work for recovery. Stairs are difficult after a tummy tuck. You need room service or easy food access, good blackout curtains, easy bathroom access, and a pool for later-stage recovery. A serviced apartment or accessible hotel within 15 minutes of the hospital is the right solution.

Minimum Bangkok stay before flying home: 10–14 days for rhinoplasty or eyelid surgery. 2–3 weeks for abdominoplasty or breast augmentation. 10–12 days for liposuction.

## Complications Happen the Same Way Everywhere

Complications at accredited hospitals are uncommon. When they do occur, they look the same as they would at home: infection, asymmetry, revision needs. The difference is you are not home when it happens.

Manageable with the right preparation: travel insurance that covers cosmetic complications (not all policies do, ask specifically), telemedicine access to your Bangkok surgeon after you return, and clear red-flag instructions for your home GP.
