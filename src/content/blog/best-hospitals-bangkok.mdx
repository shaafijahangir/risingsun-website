Bangkok hospitals cost 50–70% less than the US or UK, are internationally accredited, and have shorter wait times than most Western systems. I have sent patients to three of them consistently for years. Here is what each one is actually good for.

## Bumrungrad International Hospital

Bumrungrad is the largest and most internationally recognised. Located in Sukhumvit, it sees over 1.1 million patients a year from 190+ countries.

**Best for:** Complex diagnostics, cancer treatment, cardiac care, joint replacement, second opinions.

**What sets it apart:** Scale and depth. 580 beds, 30+ specialist centres, on-site interpreter services in 30+ languages. Waiting times are short. Senior physicians have typically trained in the UK, US, or Australia.

**Typical costs:** Full cardiac checkup package: $200–350. Hip replacement: $12,000–16,000 all-in, versus $40,000–50,000 in the US.

## Samitivej Sukhumvit Hospital

Samitivej is more intimate than Bumrungrad, with the same standard of care. Its paediatric wing is among the best in Southeast Asia.

**Best for:** Families with children, fertility treatments, orthopaedics, executive health screenings.

**What sets it apart:** Patient experience. Quieter rooms, higher staff ratios, and health screening packages that are genuinely comprehensive.

**Typical costs:** Executive health screening from $180. IVF packages from $4,500–6,000 per cycle.

## BangPakok 9 International Hospital

BangPakok 9 delivers identical outcomes for many procedures at lower prices. It is my first recommendation for orthopaedic and minimally invasive surgery on a tighter budget.

**Best for:** Orthopaedic surgery, laparoscopic procedures, dental work, cosmetic surgery.

**What sets it apart:** Price transparency. Fixed-price packages for most procedures are published on request, making budgeting straightforward.

**Typical costs:** Knee replacement: $7,000–10,000. Dental implants: $800–1,200 per implant.
