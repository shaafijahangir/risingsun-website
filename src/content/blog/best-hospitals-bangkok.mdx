Bangkok is one of the world's leading destinations for medical tourism — and for good reason. World-class hospitals, internationally trained doctors, and costs that run 50–70% lower than the US or UK have drawn over a million medical tourists a year for the past two decades.

After 20+ years helping international visitors navigate Bangkok's healthcare system, these are the three hospitals I recommend most often, and why.

## Bumrungrad International Hospital

Bumrungrad is the name most travellers hear first, and its reputation is well-earned. Located in the heart of Sukhumvit, it sees over 1.1 million patients a year from more than 190 countries.

**Best for:** Complex diagnostics, cancer treatment, cardiac care, joint replacement, and second opinions on serious diagnoses.

**What sets it apart:** The scale. Bumrungrad operates like a small city — 580 beds, 30+ specialist centres, and an in-house hotel-style concierge for international patients. Waiting times are short and interpreter services cover 30+ languages.

**Typical costs:** A full cardiac checkup package runs around $200–350 USD. Hip replacement is roughly $12,000–16,000 USD all-in, compared to $40,000–50,000 in the United States.

## Samitivej Sukhumvit Hospital

Samitivej feels more intimate than Bumrungrad while maintaining the same standard of care. It is particularly strong in paediatrics — the children's wing is among the best in Southeast Asia.

**Best for:** Families travelling with children, fertility treatments, orthopaedics, and executive health screenings.

**What sets it apart:** Patient experience. Rooms are quieter, staff-to-patient ratios are higher, and the executive health screening packages are genuinely comprehensive — not the watered-down versions you see elsewhere.

**Typical costs:** Executive health screening starts from $180 USD. IVF packages start at approximately $4,500–6,000 USD per cycle.

## BangPakok 9 International Hospital

BangPakok 9 is the best-value option of the three, and for many procedures it delivers identical outcomes at meaningfully lower prices. It has a strong reputation for orthopaedic surgery and minimally invasive procedures.

**Best for:** Orthopaedic surgery, laparoscopic procedures, dental work, and cosmetic surgery.

**What sets it apart:** Price transparency. BangPakok 9 publishes fixed-price packages for most common procedures, which makes budgeting straightforward. The international patient desk is responsive and handles coordination efficiently.

**Typical costs:** Knee replacement is typically $7,000–10,000 USD. Dental implants run $800–1,200 per implant.
