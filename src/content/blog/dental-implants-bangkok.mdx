This is not a different standard of care. It is a different cost structure: lower overheads, lower labour costs, lower property costs in Thailand. The clinical outcome is the same.

## What the Process Actually Involves

Dental implants are not a single appointment. The full process takes 3–6 months, which surprises some people and matters for planning your trip.

**Stage 1: Consultation and extraction (if needed)**
First appointment covers X-rays, 3D CT scan, assessment of bone density, and treatment planning. If you have a tooth that needs extraction before implant placement, this is done at the same appointment or the next day. Cost: typically included in the implant package.

**Stage 2: Implant placement**
The titanium post is placed into the jawbone under local anaesthetic. This is a 45–90 minute procedure. You leave the same day. Mild discomfort for 3–5 days, manageable with standard pain relief.

**Stage 3: Osseointegration**
The implant fuses with the bone. This takes 3–6 months and happens whether you are in Bangkok or back home. You do not need to stay in Thailand for this stage.

**Stage 4: Crown placement**
The final crown is fitted. This requires one appointment. Many dental tourists combine this with a return trip to Thailand — or in some cases, have the crown placed by their home dentist using the Bangkok abutment components and detailed specifications provided.

**For most people, the practical approach is:**
- Trip 1 to Bangkok: Consultation + implant placement (3–5 days total)
- Wait 3–6 months at home
- Trip 2 to Bangkok (or home dentist): Crown placement

Some patients combine Trip 1 with a holiday. Some wait until they have multiple dental procedures to make a single trip worthwhile.

## Same-Trip Options

For patients who cannot make two trips, immediate loading implants — where the crown is placed at the same time as the implant — are available for suitable candidates. Not everyone qualifies (bone density must meet specific criteria), but it is an option worth discussing at your consultation.

Full-mouth rehabilitation on the same trip is also possible if you are replacing multiple teeth with a fixed bridge rather than individual implants. All-on-4 and All-on-6 procedures (full arch implant-supported bridges) are performed at both hospitals listed above and can often be completed within 5–7 days including healing time.

## What to Bring From Home

Your Bangkok dentist will take their own X-rays and CT scan. But bringing existing dental records, recent X-rays, and any previous treatment notes helps the consultation move faster and may reveal relevant history (previous root canals, bone grafts, failed implants) that affects treatment planning.

If you have had previous implants that failed, bring the records. Bumrungrad handles implant failure cases regularly.

## The Recovery Window

Implant placement recovery is mild. Most patients are comfortable for sightseeing and restaurant meals within 24–48 hours. Avoid hard foods for the first week. No smoking (it significantly affects osseointegration and implant success rates — your surgeon will ask about this).

You can fly home 24–48 hours after implant placement with no clinical risk.
